📖 Weekly Reflections

Week 01
Role of CS Graduate

What I Learned: A Computer Science graduate is much more than just a programmer. We are problem solvers, ethical thinkers, and lifelong learners. Technical skills matter, but so do communication, integrity, and teamwork.

Key Insight: Regular self assessment helps us recognize our strengths and the areas where we need to grow.

Real life Application: This encouraged me to honestly evaluate my own skills and create a realistic personal development plan.

Week 02
CS in Society

What I Learned: Computing has completely reshaped how we learn, work, and communicate. But with this power comes responsibility. Issues like the digital divide and data privacy are real and urgent.

Key Insight: Every technological solution we build has social consequences, whether good or bad.

Real life Application: I now try to think about the broader impact of my projects before jumping into code.

Week 03
Workplace Culture

What I Learned: A healthy workplace is not just about following rules. It is built on respect, diversity, and inclusion. When people feel safe and valued, teams perform much better.

Key Insight: Professional conduct means treating everyone fairly, not just checking off a list.

Real life Application: I keep these principles in mind during group projects and university events.

Week 04
Personal Branding

What I Learned: Your online presence, including LinkedIn, GitHub, and even how you write emails, is often someone's first impression of you. A degree alone is not enough anymore.

Key Insight: Building a personal brand means consistently showing your skills, values, and professionalism.

Real life Application: I have started updating my LinkedIn and GitHub with my projects and certifications.

Week 05
Codes of Conduct

What I Learned: Professional organizations like ACM, IEEE, and BCS have clear codes of conduct. They remind us that honesty, public safety, and user privacy are not optional. These are the foundation of trust in technology.

Key Insight: Ethics is not a side note in computing. It is a core responsibility that every professional must take seriously.

Real life Application: Whenever I face an ethical dilemma in a project, I now ask myself what these codes would guide me to do.

Week 06
Lifelong Learning

What I Learned: Technology changes faster than any degree can keep up with. What we learn today might evolve tomorrow, so staying curious and continuously updating our skills is essential.

Key Insight: Graduation is not the end of learning. It is where professional growth really begins.

Real life Application: I am actively working on security certifications and trying to stay updated with industry trends.

Week 07
Ethics in Computing

What I Learned: From AI bias to data leaks and responsible disclosure, ethics in computing is not abstract. The systems we build affect real people, and we are accountable for their safety and fairness.

Key Insight: Even small technical choices can have serious ethical consequences.

Real life Application: I now try to apply privacy by design in every project, thinking about user safety from the very first step.
